Summary: Vittara, founded by Darshik Pratapbhai Fataniya, is committed to protecting your personal data. We process data in accordance with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. Our team is based in India but all services are directed at UK clients.

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Vittara ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal information when you use our website, engage our services, or communicate with our team.

We are committed to handling your data responsibly and in full compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

2. Who We Are

Vittara is a financial services firm founded and operated by Darshik Pratapbhai Fataniya, headquartered in Porbandar, Gujarat, India. We source and serve UK-based individual and business clients, delivering accounting, taxation, paraplanning, and advisory services.

Data Controller: Darshik Pratapbhai Fataniya (trading as Vittara) · Email: privacy@vittara.co.uk · Address: Porbandar, Gujarat, India

3. Data We Collect

Information you provide directly

  • Name, email address, and telephone number (via contact or booking forms)
  • Business name, company registration number, UTR number
  • Financial records, tax documents, and bank statements shared for our services
  • National Insurance number and date of birth (where required for HMRC filing)
  • Payment information (processed through secure third-party providers)

Information collected automatically

  • IP address, browser type, and device information
  • Pages visited, time on site, and referral source
  • Cookie data (see section 11)

Information from third parties

  • HMRC data shared as part of authorised tax return processes
  • Accounting software data (Xero, QuickBooks) when you grant access

4. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data to:

  • Provide accounting, tax, paraplanning, and advisory services
  • Communicate with you about your account, services, and appointments
  • Prepare and file tax returns, VAT returns, and financial statements with HMRC
  • Send service updates, deadline reminders, and relevant financial insights
  • Comply with our legal and regulatory obligations
  • Improve our website and service quality
  • Send marketing communications (only with your consent — you may opt out at any time)

5. Legal Basis for Processing

We rely on the following legal bases under UK GDPR:

  • Contract performance — processing necessary to deliver your contracted services
  • Legal obligation — compliance with HMRC, FCA, and other regulatory requirements
  • Legitimate interests — improving our services, fraud prevention, security
  • Consent — for marketing emails and non-essential cookies (you may withdraw consent at any time)

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your data with:

  • HMRC — as required for tax filing and compliance
  • FCA-regulated bodies — where paraplanning services require it
  • Cloud accounting platforms — Xero, QuickBooks (under data processing agreements)
  • Professional advisers — where we need legal, audit, or specialist input
  • IT service providers — for secure hosting, email, and website infrastructure

All third parties are required to handle your data securely and in accordance with applicable law.

7. International Data Transfers

As a UK-focused firm operating from India, some of your data will be processed in India. We ensure all international transfers are protected by appropriate safeguards, including standard contractual clauses approved by the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

8. Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including legal and regulatory requirements:

  • Tax records: 6 years from the end of the relevant tax year (HMRC requirement)
  • Financial records: 6 years from the date of the last transaction
  • Marketing consent data: Until you withdraw consent or request deletion
  • Website usage data: Up to 24 months

9. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure — request deletion of your data (subject to legal obligations)
  • Right to restriction — request we limit processing in certain circumstances
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent — at any time, for consent-based processing

To exercise any of these rights, please email privacy@vittara.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the ICO at ico.org.uk.

10. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data in transit
  • Encrypted storage for sensitive financial documents
  • Access controls — only authorised team members can access client data
  • Regular security reviews and staff training

11. Cookies

We use cookies to improve your experience on our website. For full details, please see our Cookie Policy.

12. Children

Our services are not directed at children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us immediately.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via email or a prominent notice on our website. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
